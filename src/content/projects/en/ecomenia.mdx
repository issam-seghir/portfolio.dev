## Project Overview

[**Ecomenia**](https://ecomenia.com) is an **all-in-one e-commerce management platform** for growing merchants: **orders**, **deliveries**, **inventory**, **teams**, and **multi-store** operations—**centralized dashboards**, **real-time tracking**, and **integrations** that replace a patchwork of tools.

I co-founded the product and **owned the technical architecture and implementation** end to end: from core SaaS foundations to shipping features businesses rely on daily. The codebase is **proprietary**; there is **no public repository**. A founder perspective on the collaboration appears on the [About](/about) page.

## The Problem

E-commerce operators in **MENA** and similar markets often juggle **COD workflows**, **local carriers**, **WhatsApp-led sales**, and **multiple storefronts**—yet generic Western tooling rarely matches how inventory, payouts, and teams actually work on the ground.

## The Solution

### Operations and orders

- **Unified order lifecycle** from capture to fulfillment—**real-time status**, handoffs to logistics, and visibility for staff.
- **Multi-source orders** (e.g. web, marketplace-style flows) with **structured** handling so teams do not lose signal as volume grows.

### Delivery and partners

- **Carrier integrations** such as **Yalidine** and **Mystro** so shipping is **first-class**, not bolted on at the last mile.
- Workflows that reflect **local delivery** expectations: tracking, milestones, and fewer manual follow-ups.

### Inventory, products, and channels

- **Inventory** with alerts, trends, and controls suited to **high-SKU** catalogs and day-to-day merchandising.
- **Store platform connectors** (e.g. **Shopify**, **WooCommerce**, **YouCan**) to **import** and **sync** catalog data instead of retyping reality.

### Teams and access

- **Role-based permissions**, **team** structure, and **activity** patterns that stay understandable as orgs scale beyond a handful of people.

### Experience and reach

- **Arabic / English** experiences with **RTL** where the product calls for it, so interfaces match how teams actually work.

### Engineering approach

- A **modern TypeScript** stack (**Next.js**, **tRPC**, **Prisma**, **PostgreSQL**) with **shadcn/ui** and **Tailwind CSS** for **fast**, **accessible** dashboards—structured so features stay **maintainable** as the product surface grows.

## What I Learned

Building **operator-grade SaaS** means designing for **messy real-world operations**—COD, carriers, WhatsApp-heavy funnels, and permissioned teams—not just idealized checkout demos. The hard part is **clarity under load**: systems that stay predictable when real money and real shipments move through them every day.
